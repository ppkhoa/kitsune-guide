    Audio Comparison Guide¶

    This guide explains how to create spectrograms, waveforms, dynamic range statistics, and browser-playable audio previews for comparing different audio encodes such as AAC, AC-3, E-AC-3 / DDP, DTS, or lossless formats.

    Requirements¶

    Software¶

    • SoX — for spectrograms and amplitude statistics
    • FFmpeg — for converting audio to FLAC and other formats
    • eac3to — optional, for Blu-ray audio demuxing
    • Audacity — for waveform visualization and screenshots
    • foobar2000 + DR Meter component — for generating dynamic range logs

    Step 1 — Convert Audio to FLAC (Required)¶

    Spectrograms and stats work best on PCM/FLAC.

    ffmpeg -i input.eac3 -sample_fmt s16 -ar 48000 output.flac
ffmpeg -i input.aac  -sample_fmt s16 -ar 48000 output.flac

    Step 2 — Generate Spectrograms with SoX¶

    PowerShell (one line):

    sox input.flac -S -n spectrogram -x 3000 -y 513 -z 120 -w Kaiser -t "Spectrogram" -o output_spectrogram.png

    Step 3 — Generate Waveforms (Audacity)¶

    1. Drag FLAC into Audacity
    2. Enable clipping overlay
      View → Show Clipping
    3. Fit visually
      View → Track Size → Fit to Height
    4. Export PNG screenshot

    Step 4 — SoX Amplitude Statistics¶

    sox input.flac -n stats 2> stats.txt

    Step 5 — DR Meter Dynamic Range Logs¶

    1. Load FLAC into foobar2000
    2. Right-click → Dynamic Range Meter
    3. Save log output
    4. Combine with SoX stats

    Step 6 — Upload to Slow.pics¶

    Upload: - Spectrograms
    - Waveforms
    - SoX stats screenshot
    - DR logs screenshot

    slow.pics
