    Streaming Services¶

    Goal: Provide a quick reference for streaming service tags used in WEB-DL release naming.

    What You'll Learn

    • Standard release group tags for streaming services
    • How to identify the correct tag for your source
    • Comprehensive list of services and their abbreviations

    1. About Streaming Tags¶

    When naming a WEB-DL release, the streaming service tag identifies where the content was sourced from. This helps viewers and trackers understand the origin and quality expectations.

    Example naming format: 

    Movie.Name.2024.2160p.AMZN.WEB-DL.DDP5.1.H.265-GROUP
                      ^^^^
                      Service tag

    2. Common Services Quick Reference¶

    Service Tag Notes
    Amazon Prime Video AMZN Most common WEB-DL source
    Netflix NF High quality, often has DV/HDR
    Disney+ DSNP Marvel, Star Wars, Disney content
    Apple TV+ ATVP Original content only
    HBO Max / Max HMAX / MAX Premium content
    Paramount+ PMTP CBS, Paramount content
    Peacock PCOK NBC/Universal content
    Hulu HULU US streaming service
    Crunchyroll CR Major anime source, simulcasts, subs
    Funimation FUNI Anime releases, legacy catalog
    Discovery+ DSCP Documentaries, lifestyle, factual

    Service Rebranding

    Some services have been rebranded:

    • HBO Max → Max → HBO Max (use HMAX for new content)
    • DPlay → Discovery+ (use DSCP)
    • Funimation → Crunchyroll (use CR for new content)
